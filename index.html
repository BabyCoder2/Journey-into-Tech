Breaking Into Tech

In Need of Change

It was October 2021 when I was looking for a different career path. I was unsure what I wanted to do but I knew that working as a call center rep was not the ideal career path for me. Being on the phone with such entitled people really urked my soul that I started to not care anymore. Compassion fatigue is what it was. It was time for a shift.

I started doing some research into tech positions and narrowed it down to non phone tech jobs. That is where I was introduced to web development. This was the first time in my life that I ever heard about web development and software engineer. Very first time and pondered on why in the hell am I just now learning about this stuff. So I started Youtubing for answers and came across alot of channels.

Who Do I Turn To?

Youtubing how to get into the tech space lead me down a road that was very unhelpful. The information were so conflicting. Some were not giving any information meaning they were holding back or fearmongoring to some of the channels were saying why you shouldnt get into this positions. It was so discouraging and that's where I questioned was this even something I wanted to do. I paid for subscriptions and didn't finish, I stopped only to venture off into seeing if QA was something I wanted to do instead. Never sought that bootcamp to completion. I actually had the blessing to get a scholarship to participate in Udacity's Frontend development course and I didn't complete that...

The Breaking Point

I evenutally just stopped and starting focusing on work and still questioned was this even something I wanted to do. It in fact was, I just didnt have guidance and had too many distractions around me. I had to ask myself what was holding me back from completing these courses. 1. I really didn't have the extra finances to invest in a course 2. Setting boundaries with people to let it be known I am not available as much as I used to be.

Those 2 things really were the main issues. So I decided to go back to the very beginning. Meaning,my very first course I bought was Colt Steeles complete web development course on udemy. I evenutally this year (2024) came across roadmap.sh where I was introduced to Dave Gray. So here you have Dave Gray a profressor and is giving out free content and Colt who has been teaching a bootcamp where students have gone off to get jobs. Surely I am in good hands. Right?

The way I see it...I wont know if it works if I dont give it a chance.
